Communicating Data Findings of Ford Go Bikes¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this presentation, I will be investigating factors from a history of Ford Go Bikes rides. I will be using trip data as well as demographics to find how who uses the bikes and for what.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset is from a history of all Ford Go Bike rides in California. The data includes trip data like distance and duration, demographics such as gender and age and much more.

The Relationship of Gender and Age by Bike Ride Frequency¶

The relationship between gender and age by bike ride frequency is right skewed. There is a high incline towards the younger ages and a slower, more steady decline when reaching the older ages. Females have one peak at around age 30 where they are 2 proportions higher than males. Males have two peaks at around ages 25 and 35. It also looks like males are more likely to go on bike rides into their older years than females.

The Relationship of Distance and Time by Age Group¶

The visual shows the relationship of distance and time by age groups. It is clearly seen that age group 26-35 is the most active with distance and time. There is in overall positve trend with stronger correlation torwards the smaller number and exapnding as the number grow larger. The older age groups of 46-55 and 56+ seem be take shorter rides compared to other age groups. It also looks like the younger age group of 15-25 take longer rides in time but shorter rides in distance than other age groups. All in all, bike rides that are under 1.5 miles and 20 minutes have strong positive correlation.
